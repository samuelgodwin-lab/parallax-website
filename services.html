FAQ

Questions
we hear often.

Straight answers to the things clients ask before they engage with us.

How long does a typical project take?

+

It depends on scope, but most projects fall into one of three bands: a focused sprint (2–4 weeks), a mid-scope engagement (6–12 weeks), or a full partnership (3–6 months). We'll give you a clear timeline after our first conversation — no vague estimates.

Do you work with early-stage startups?

+

Yes — some of our best work has been with pre-seed and seed-stage founders. What matters isn't the size of the company but the clarity of the problem and the quality of the thinking. If you've got both, we want to talk.

Can we engage you for just one discipline?

+

Absolutely. You can come to us with just a brand brief, just an engineering problem, or just a motion design challenge. We don't require you to buy a full suite — though many clients end up expanding the engagement once the first piece of work lands.

Do you offer ongoing retainers?

+

Yes. For clients who want a consistent design and engineering partner rather than a one-off project, we offer monthly retainer arrangements. These give you a reserved slice of our capacity, a direct line to the team, and faster turnarounds. Ask us about availability.

What does your first conversation look like?

+

A 30-minute call, no pitch, no pressure. We want to understand the problem you're trying to solve — not sell you on a service before we know if it fits. If there's a genuine match, we'll follow up with a clear proposal within 48 hours.
